Chickpea Curry

Description

This recipe is great for two reasons, the first being that it is very simple to make and the second being that it is easy to batch cook and freeze for future meals!

Instead of using individual ingredients for seasoning, curry powder is used for simplicity. Canned chickpeas are used for the same reason. Only onion and spinach are mentioned as vegetable ingredients, however any vegetables can be added such as mushrooms or bell pepper.

Ingredients

Serves 8

  • Oil
  • 2 Onions
  • Curry Powder
  • Tomato Puree
  • 2 x 400g Cans of Chickpeas
  • 2 x 400g Can of Chopped Tomatoes
  • Spinach
  • Rice (1/2 cup per serving)

Steps

30 minutes

  1. Heat the oil in a large pan on medium-high heat and boil the kettle for the rice
  2. Boil the rice in double the amount of water in a covered saucepan on medium-low heat. Check back often as the rice is ready when all of the water has boiled away
  3. Chop the onions and add to the pan for around 8 minutes. Chop and add any other vegetables
  4. Add the curry powder and tomato puree, and then heat for 2 minutes to create a paste. Stir occasionally to avoid sticking
  5. Drain the canned chickpeas and add to the pan with the chopped tomatoes. Simmer for 10 minutes
  6. Add the spinach and cook until they wilt
  7. Serve the curry on top of the rice
